Reading the table

Mudah inversion: 40% of all enquiries, zero tenancies in six months. Cheap leads are not cheap if they never sign.
Google pays off: the most expensive enquiry (RM31) becomes the second-cheapest paid tenancy (RM1,800), because half its leads are qualified.
Action: formalize a referral ask at every signing; cap Meta to room campaigns; treat Mudah as free background noise, never paid boosts.

What the curve means

Week 1 to 2: SV2 attracted 29 enquiries, healthy curiosity for a premium tower.
Week 3 onward: velocity collapsed roughly 70% and never recovered, while Nexus at RM2,999 kept moving and signed in May.
Recommendation: the market has answered the RM400 premium. Reprice toward RM3,000 to 3,100 or add value (parking, flexible terms).

The after-hours leak

Unanswered rate by time slot

Recommendation

One in five enquiries arrives after 8pm or on weekends, when renters actually browse, and one in five of those never gets a reply.
Fix: WhatsApp auto-acknowledgement with three qualifying questions (move-in date, occupants, viewing availability) holds the lead overnight at zero cost. Recovering half the leak is worth roughly RM350 in paid leads plus the referral and portal leads on top.
