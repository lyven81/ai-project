# How to Use Ecommerce Analyst

## What This App Does
Ecommerce Analyst is your private analyst for your online store. Upload your sales data and ask it any question — "which category is losing money?", "is my discount actually worth it?", "which state should I stop shipping to?" It answers in plain English, grounded in your own numbers. Every Monday morning it also writes you a short brief so you know what's working and what's bleeding before the week starts.

## How to Start
1. Double-click `open-app.bat` to open the app in your browser.
2. On the **Upload** screen, either drag in your platform transaction export (CSV) or click **Try with sample data** to see how it works.
3. Wait a few seconds while the app reads your data. Your file stays on your own instance — no one else sees it.
4. Land on the **Dashboard + Chat** screen. Start by clicking one of the 6 quick-action buttons on the left, or type a question at the bottom.

## How to Use It
- **Quick questions:** click any of the 6 buttons — "What's eating my margin?", "Which state is unprofitable?", "Are my discounts working?", etc. The app replies in a paragraph with numbers.
- **Ask anything:** type a free-form question in the chat input at the bottom. Keep it plain — "which 5 products have the worst return rate?" works better than technical phrasing.
- **Monday Brief:** every Monday 7am, the app prepares a new weekly brief — 3 wins, 3 worries, 1 decision. Click it to read, click any finding to dig into the chat.
- **History:** every answer you've seen is saved in the History tab. Search it by keyword or date. Export as PDF if you want to share with your accountant or team.

## Tips
- Drop your CSV **weekly** for freshest insights. The Monday brief uses the last 7 days.
- If an answer surprises you, ask **"why?"** — the analyst will break down the number.
- The app only knows what's in your CSV. It can't see your ad spend or inventory. Those are separate conversations.

## Saving Your Work
Everything is saved automatically in your own app instance. No account needed. Your CSV is never uploaded anywhere outside your instance. If you're using the web demo, your session clears when you close the tab — for permanent history, use the live Cloud Run version.
