# Recipe

Serves 4. Total time: ~2 hours (30 mins if using store-bought paste).

## Phase 1 — Make the Paste

*Skip if using store-bought paste (Cap Helang or Cap Ibu recommended).*

1. **Dry toast the spices.** Toast 2 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp fennel until fragrant (2–3 min). Grind to powder.
2. **Soak dried ingredients.** Soak 15 dried chilies and 3 tbsp dried prawns in warm water for 20 minutes. Drain.
3. **Blend the paste.** Combine all ingredients with a splash of water. Blend until smooth.
4. **Fry the paste.** Heat 4 tbsp oil. Fry paste for 15–20 minutes, stirring constantly, until darkened and oil separates. Do not rush.

## Phase 2 — Build the Broth

5. **Make prawn stock.** Fry prawn heads in oil until red. Add 1.5L water, simmer 20 min. Strain.
6. **Add stock.** Pour prawn stock + 500ml chicken stock into the pot with fried paste. Simmer 15 min.
7. **Add coconut milk.** Reduce to low. Add 400ml coconut milk. Do not boil. Season with salt and sugar.

## Phase 3 — Prepare Toppings

8. **Cook chicken.** Poach 2 breasts in salted water for 15 min. Shred while warm.
9. **Cook prawns.** Poach 16 medium prawns in broth for 2–3 min. Peel.
10. **Make egg strips.** Thin omelette from 3 eggs. Roll and slice into ribbons.
11. **Blanch noodles.** Bee hoon in boiling water for 30 seconds. Drain.

## Phase 4 — Assemble

12. **Build the bowl.** Noodles → ladle hot broth → prawns, chicken, egg strips on top.
13. **Garnish.** Fresh coriander, lime wedge, sambal belacan on side. Serve immediately.

## Pro Tips

- The paste needs 15–20 full minutes of frying, not 5.
- Never boil after adding coconut milk.
- Use prawn stock, not plain water.
- Coriander is a flavour layer, not decoration.
- Squeeze lime just before eating.
