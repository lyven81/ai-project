# Ingredients

The soul of Sarawak Laksa is its paste — a hand-pounded or blended mix of 15+ aromatics. Everything else builds on that foundation.

## The Sambal Laksa Paste

- **Lemongrass** (6–8 stalks) — citrusy, floral fragrance
- **Galangal** (thumb-sized) — earthier and more medicinal than ginger
- **Garlic** (6 cloves) — aromatic backbone
- **Shallots** (10) — sweetness and depth
- **Dried prawns** (3 tbsp, soaked) — umami without fishiness
- **Dried chilies** (15, soaked) — colour and mild heat
- **Candlenuts** (6) — thickens paste; macadamia as substitute
- **Belacan** (1 tsp, toasted) — fermented shrimp paste; do not skip
- **Coriander seeds, cumin, fennel** — dry-toasted and ground; warmth without heat

## The Broth

- **Coconut milk** (1 can, 400ml) — added at the end; do not boil after adding
- **Prawn stock** — made from prawn heads/shells; the true broth base
- **Chicken stock** — blended 50/50 with prawn stock

## Toppings & Garnish

- **Bee hoon** (rice vermicelli) — the only correct noodle
- **Prawns** — poached whole, peeled, placed on top
- **Shredded chicken** — poached breast, hand-shredded while warm
- **Egg omelette strips** — thin omelette sliced into ribbons
- **Fresh coriander** — non-negotiable; use a full handful
- **Lime wedge** — squeeze in just before eating
