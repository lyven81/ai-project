# Regional Variations

Sarawak Laksa is not one fixed recipe — it shifts across the state's towns.

## Kuching Style (The Standard)
The benchmark. Reddish-orange broth, lightly herbal, clean coconut milk finish. Bee hoon only. Prawns, shredded chicken, egg strips. Coriander mandatory. Every other version is judged against this.

## Sibu Style
Slightly sweeter — a nod to Sibu's Foochow Chinese community. More coconut milk, making the broth creamier and paler. Less assertive spice.

## Miri Style
Closer to Kuching but some stalls add torch ginger flower (bunga kantan) as garnish, giving a floral edge. Influenced slightly by Brunei's richer laksa traditions.

## Home Style
Made with store-bought paste (Cap Helang or Cap Ibu). Families personalise — extra galangal, more coconut milk, dried tofu puffs added. The version most Sarawakians grew up eating.

## Modern / Restaurant Style
Upscale interpretations in KL and Singapore. Sometimes with tiger prawns, sous-vide chicken, or truffle oil. Purists dismiss these, but they introduce the dish to a wider audience.
