# Where to Eat

The best Sarawak Laksa is eaten at hawker stalls before 9am.

## Top Picks

### Chong Choon Cafe
- **Location:** Jalan Wan Alwi, Kuching
- **Hours:** 7:00am – 11:30am
- **Halal:** No
- One of Kuching's most celebrated laksa stalls. Deep, toasted spice broth balanced with coconut milk. Queue starts at 7am. Often sold out by 10am.

### Dahlia's Sarawak Laksa
- **Location:** Jalan Datuk Ajibah Abol, Kuching
- **Hours:** 7:30am – 12:00pm
- **Halal:** Yes
- Run by a Malay family. House-made paste, generous prawns, slightly richer coconut finish. Popular with locals and hotel guests.

## Other Good Options

### Kedai Kopi Hock Seng
- Padungan area. Classic Kuching style, no frills, fair price. The kopi is excellent.
- Hours: 6:30am – 11:00am | Non-halal

### Golden Arch Cafe
- Jalan Chan Chin Ann. Popular with office workers. High turnover = always fresh broth.
- Hours: 7:00am – 10:30am | Non-halal

### Satok Weekend Market
- Jalan Satok. Saturday and Sunday mornings only. Casual, authentic, atmospheric.
- Hours: Sat–Sun 6:00am – 10:00am | Mixed

### Ban Hock Road Hawkers
- Multiple vendors near the waterfront. Not the best single bowl, but great atmosphere.
- Hours: 7:00am – 12:00pm | Mixed

## Tip
Most stalls close on certain weekdays. Visit Saturday morning for the best chance of finding multiple stalls open. Always arrive before 9am.
