# The Role of Youth

"One of the most striking and inspiring features of this twenty-five-year period has been the service rendered by Bahá'í youth, who with faith and valour have assumed their rightful place in the forefront of the community's efforts." — UHJ, Riḍván 2021

## Lowering the Voting Age
At Riḍván 2021, the voting age in Bahá'í elections was lowered from 21 to **18**. The age for serving on a Spiritual Assembly remains 21.

## Forms of Service
- **Teachers of the Cause** — study circle tutors, children's class teachers
- **Animators** — facilitators of junior youth groups
- **Homefront pioneers** — moving within their country to open new localities
- **Cluster coordinators** — institutional roles in advancing growth
- **Auxiliary Board agencies** — serving the protection and propagation of the Faith

## Institute for Studies in Global Prosperity
Graduate and undergraduate seminars exploring academic disciplines in light of Bahá'í teachings. Serves youth from 100+ countries. Builds capacity to contribute to public life.

## Junior Youth as Protagonists
Ages 11–14. Not just recipients of education — active agents of community transformation. The junior youth programme develops powers of expression, moral reasoning, and commitment to service.
