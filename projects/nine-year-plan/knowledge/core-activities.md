# Core Activities

Four activities form the heartbeat of Bahá'í community life. Together they generate the dynamics of growth.

## 1. Study Circles
Groups studying the Ruhi Institute sequence. Build spiritual qualities, knowledge, and skills for service. The movement of individuals through courses propels cluster development.

## 2. Children's Classes
Spiritual education nurturing moral character and love of the divine teachings. Aim: reach every child in a village or neighbourhood.
- Materials: Ruhi Books 3 and 3A
- Teachers trained through the institute process

## 3. Devotional Meetings
Gatherings centred on prayer and sacred writings. Open to all. Transform the spiritual atmosphere of a neighbourhood and serve as a first point of encounter with the wider society.

## 4. Junior Youth Groups
Programme for ages 11–14 with "extraordinary potential." Animators trained through the institute guide junior youth in developing powers of expression, moral reasoning, and commitment to service.
- Materials: Ruhi Book 5
- Junior youth are active agents, not passive recipients

## The Institute Process
When the four activities are woven together, the dynamics generated give rise to a vibrant pattern of community life. They are deeply interconnected — each strengthening the others.
