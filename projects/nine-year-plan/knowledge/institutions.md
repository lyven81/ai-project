# Institutions

## Universal House of Justice (International)
Supreme governing body. Elected every five years. Issues global Plans from the Bahá'í World Centre in Haifa, Israel.

## Continental Boards of Counsellors
Appointed bodies serving propagation and protection across continents. Their December 2021 conference at the World Centre deliberated the Nine Year Plan. Work closely with NSAs to systematize learning.

## National Spiritual Assemblies (National)
Elected annually in each country. Oversee training institutes, appoint Regional Councils and National Growth Committees, guide Local Assemblies.

## Regional Bahá'í Councils (Regional)
Established in 230 regions. Proved "indispensable for advancing the process of growth." Where Councils develop enhanced capacity, whole regions accelerate. The Nine Year Plan calls for more systematic support to Local Assemblies.

## Local Spiritual Assemblies (Local)
Elected annually where 9+ adult Bahá'ís reside. During the Nine Year Plan, LSAs take greater responsibility for community development — managing local funds, supporting social action, interacting with civil society. A two-stage electoral process is being authorized in more places.

## Auxiliary Board Members (Supporting)
Appointed by Continental Boards of Counsellors. Serve protection and propagation at the grassroots. "Closely connected to conditions at the grassroots." Help communities overcome prejudice, strengthen unity, and maintain focus.

## National Growth Committee
A new structure being established in smaller countries without Regional Councils. Three, five, or seven members. Appointed by the NSA to foster movement of clusters, support teaching projects, and coordinate with the training institute.
