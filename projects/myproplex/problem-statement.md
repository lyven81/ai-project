# Problem Statement — Malaysian Property Law Research Assistant

## The Problem

Malaysian property lawyers and conveyancing clerks spend 2–4 hours every working day doing manual legal research. They search through case law databases, read legislation updates, and cross-check multiple sources before they can advise a single client. This is slow, expensive, and error-prone.

The core issues:
- Legal databases (CLJ, MLJ) are expensive and require manual searching
- Malaysian property legislation changes regularly — amendments are easy to miss
- Junior lawyers spend billable hours on research that should take minutes
- Missing one case precedent or one legislative update can expose the firm to liability

## Who Has This Problem

- Property law firms (small to mid-size)
- Conveyancing departments in larger law firms
- Independent conveyancing lawyers
- Legal consultants advising property developers

## What They Currently Do

They manually search CLJ, Google, Bar Council publications, and the Attorney General's Chambers website. They rely on memory and printed notes for frequently cited acts. They call colleagues to double-check interpretations.

## What We Are Building

An AI-powered research assistant that specialises exclusively in Malaysian property law. It searches the web for relevant legislation, case law, and legal updates, then gives a clear, cited answer in seconds.

## The Outcome

Lawyers get research answers in under 2 minutes instead of 2 hours. They can take on more cases, reduce junior lawyer overhead, and lower the risk of missing a critical legal update.

## Key Acts Covered

- National Land Code (NLC) 1965
- Housing Development (Control & Licensing) Act (HDA) 1966
- Strata Titles Act (STA) 1985
- Real Property Gains Tax Act (RPGT) 1976
- Real Estate and Housing Developers Act (RERA)
- Specific Relief Act 1950
- Contracts Act 1950
